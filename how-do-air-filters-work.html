how Do Air Filters Work?

Air filters are devices that trap airborne particles in your home’s HVAC system. They’re essential for improving indoor air quality, reducing allergies and asthma attacks, and extending the life of your HVAC system. Air filters trap microscopic dust, pollen, mold spores, and other irritants that cause allergies and asthma attacks.Air filters come in many shapes and sizes, but they all have the same basic purpose: to clean the air of contaminants before it reaches your lungs. There are many types of air filters available to meet your specific needs and HVAC system. You can choose between different types of air filters based on the type of contaminants you want to trap in your home or office. Depending on your needs and HVAC system type, you can use either a standard filter, a HEPA filter, a UV air purifier, or a combination of these.

What are the different types of air filters?

There are two main types of air filters: mechanical filters and electrostatic filters.A mechanical filter consists of a fabric or paper medium that traps airborne contaminants. The most common type of mechanical filter is a fiberglass mesh filter. A fiberglass mesh filter is made up of tiny fibers that trap dust, pollen, mold spores, pet dander, and other contaminants in the air.A mechanical filter is effective at trapping very small particles in the air. However, it will also trap some larger particles. A mechanical filter will trap most larger contaminants in the air, but it won’t trap them as effectively as an electrostatic filter.An electrostatic filter uses an electrical charge to attract and trap contaminants in the air. It’s very effective at trapping larger and smaller particles in the air.An electrostatic filter is also effective at trapping chemicals, smoke, and odors. It’s an excellent choice for a home with pets and children, as it’s very effective at blocking allergens.

Tips for buying the right air filter

When buying a new air filter, always look for the MERV rating to determine its effectiveness. The higher the MERV rating, the more effective the filter is at trapping contaminants in the air.A good rule of thumb is to replace your air filter every one to two months. The effectiveness of your air filter will decrease over time. You should also change your air filter before you change your HVAC system.Air filters should be replaced with the same type of filter. For example, if you have a standard filter, you should replace it with a standard filter. You should never replace an air filter with a different type of filter.Air filters should be replaced based on the MERV rating. For example, a MERV 10 filter is good for removing dust and pollen from the air. A MERV 18 filter is good for removing dust, pollen, and other contaminants.
